Workshop

Privacy, Security, Safety and Ethics of AI in Pervasive Environments

Building a trustworthy, context-aware, and human-centric research agenda for embodied AI in everyday spaces.

UbiComp/ISWC 2026 Shanghai, China Hybrid Format

Overview

As AI moves from screen-based interfaces into smart homes, wearables, social robots, and ambient systems, it continuously senses and acts in shared physical spaces. This workshop focuses on cross-cutting challenges at the intersection of privacy, security, physical safety, and ethics in pervasive environments.

Topics of Interest

  • Privacy-preserving sensing and bystander privacy in shared spaces
  • Usable security and privacy interaction design for embodied AI
  • Authentication resilient to generative AI and synthetic media attacks
  • Cognitive security and misinformation governance in pervasive contexts
  • Human-AI trust modeling and calibrated reliance
  • Physical-world adversarial threats and defense mechanisms
  • Safe human-AI physical interaction and behavioral alignment
  • Ethical frameworks, value alignment, auditing, and accountability
